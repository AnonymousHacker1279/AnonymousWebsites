What’s New with Raspberry
Pi 4?
The fourth generation of single-board computers
from the Raspberry Pi Foundation has arrived

It’s been just a little over a year since the last Pi was released on May 14, 2018 in
celebration of Pi Day. The Pi 3B+ was a minor upgrade in terms of specs over its
predecessor, the Pi 3B. However, the new Pi 4 is a major upgrade in terms of power and
capacity. With these upgrades, three variants of the computer can be purchased.
More RAM, more capacity:
This is one of the more noticeable changes. A black RAM chip can be seen to the right of
the processor on the new Pi. All of the Pis after the original B+ had 1 gigabyte of RAM. The
Raspberry Pi 4 has three RAM options, leading to three variants that can be purchased. It
can be bought with one, two, or four gigabytes of RAM.
With the increase in RAM, you can have a more consistent desktop experience. It is now
much more viable to use in place of your normal computer. Besides, most computers have
4 GB ram by default.
Dual 4K displays:
Yes, you heard that correctly. Two displays, and in 4K? You might have noticed two micro-
HDMI ports on the image at the top of the page, but again, in 4K? A puny credit-card sized
computer, with beautiful, high quality images? Yes, it does have two displays, and in 4K.
Even more interesting is that if you only have one display connected, you can achieve 60
FPS. If you have two displays, however, you can only get up to 30 FPS. The ability to
connect multiple screens makes it even more possible to replace your normal desktop.
USB 3.0, up to 10x faster transfer speeds:
The new Pi has two USB 3.0 ports, which can be 10 times faster than USB 2.0 ports.
There are still two USB 2.0 ports, which can help with older devices that don’t support the
newer standards. Because the Pi supports gigabit Ethernet and 2.4/5 GHz networking, you
can make a lightning fast network drive with Samba and an external hard drive.
Faster processor:
A newer processor can be found on the Pi. It runs at 1.5 GHz, which is slightly faster than
the 3B+, which was clocked at 1.4 GHz. Just as the processors since the Model 3, this
processor supports 64-bit systems. However, you won’t notice any 64-bit related
performance increases, because Raspbian, the official image for the Raspberry Pi, doesn’t
support 64-bit and is only available in 32-bit.
Compatibility:
Physical Changes:
You won’t be able to use the same power supply or HDMI cable on the Pi 4 as you would
have for the older models. The Pi 4 requires a USB-C power supply that can provide 5.1
volts at 3 amps. It is possible to use a 2.5 amp charger, if the downstream peripherals
don’t use more than 500 mA total. You also need a micro-HDMI cable. Owners of a Pi Zero
might be slightly disappointed that their mini-HDMI cable converters won’t cut it.
You will also have to purchase a new case that is designed for the Pi 4. A lot of things
moved around, so an old case won’t fit. The Ethernet port was swapped with USB ports,
there are two micro-HDMI ports, and a USB-C port. If you want to use PoE (Power over
Ethernet), you might want to look into a case that opens at the top with enough space for
the PoE hat.
Software Changes:
You will need to get a new micro-SD card and reinstall Raspbian on it. Because the
firmware changed so much, your old cards won’t work correctly and might corrupt your
install. Other than that, most of the programs that were on Stretch should work on Buster
just fine.
The Raspberry Pi Foundation has a goal to keep all versions of Raspbian compatible with
older systems, so if you can’t get a Pi 4 and want to use Raspbian Buster, you can install it
like you would for any other system.
One of our developers has been lucky enough to get a Pi 4. When we say lucky, we mean
really lucky. The demand for these is very high, and most retailers go out of stock within a
day or two, especially the 4 GB RAM variant. At the time of writing, he still doesn’t have
any of the required peripherals, because the official power supply, HDMI cable, and case
are out of stock in most places. You might be able to get one early from a supplier outside
of the US, but you will have to wait a while, pay higher shipping, and hope that it is in good
condition when it arrives. We recommend using Adafruit to purchase a Pi. Shipping is
quick and reliable, and you can be sure that you are getting a trustworthy product.
